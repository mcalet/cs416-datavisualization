Lego Set Releases Over the Years

Over the years, Lego has grown a lot, both in the number of sets and their variety. In the first 10 years, starting from 1945, Lego released an average of 100 sets. By 2020, this number had grown to over 200 sets. Since 1990, the steady increase in set releases shows how Lego continues to evolve and captivate new and old generations. Lego Themes with the Most Set Releases

This graph highlights the top 10 Lego themes with the highest number of set releases. A curious fact: more than half of the 300+ themes currently available were introduced in the last 20 years.

Lego's Largest and Most Complex Sets

This graph shows how Lego has not only increased the number of themes released over time but also made sets bigger and more challenging. The World Map set, with over 11,000 pieces, is the largest set currently available. The Eiffel Tower set, with more than 10,000 pieces, is both one of the largest and the tallest Lego sets, measuring 58.5 inches. Both of these sets were released in the last five years. This demonstrates that as Lego's popularity has grown, so has the complexity of its sets.
